Kinnaur

Introduction

Kinnaur Unmissable places in India's beautiful Kinnaur Valley: Kinnaur, one of the prettiest valleys in Himachal Pradesh if not in India, is a heady mix of rocky mountains, snow-covered peaks, and lush valleys full of fruit orchards and alpine forests. Before you get tired of the stunning mountain views, if that is even possible, you can divert your attention to the old Himachali tradition and culture that you will find in the form of interesting architecture, ancient temples and delicious local cuisines.

Whether you are looking for a calm and relaxed holiday in the hills or an adventurous one full of outdoor exploration, Kinnaur has got them all. It is a stunning destination and if you are yet to explore Kinnaur, let this list of beautiful places in Kinnaur Valley inspire you to do so.

Places to Visit

  1. Sangla Valley: This beautiful valley is home to the Sangla Kanda, a mountain range that is said to resemble a kinner Kailash. The valley is also home to the Sangla Buddhist Monastery, which is one of the oldest monasteries in Himachal Pradesh.

  2. Chitkul: This small village is located at the end of the road in Kinnaur. It is known for its picturesque setting, surrounded by apple orchards and snow-capped peaks. Chitkul is also the last inhabited village on the road to the Indo-Tibetan border.

  3. Kalpa: Kalpa: This village is known for its stunning views of the Kinnaur Kailash mountain. Kalpa is also home to a number of Buddhist temples and monasteries.

  4. Recong Peo: The headquarters of the Kinnaur district, Recong Peo is a bustling town with a number of shops and restaurants. It is also a good place to stay if you are planning on exploring the surrounding area.

  5. Nako Lake: This beautiful lake is located at an altitude of 3,600 meters above sea level. Nako Lake is a popular spot for trekking and camping.

Local Cuisine

  • Siddu: These are steamed buns made with wheat flour and stuffed with a savory lentil or potato filling. They are a staple food in Kinnauri cuisine and are often served with ghee or chutney.

  • Dham: This is a traditional thali meal that is served on a leaf plate. It typically includes rice, dal, rajma, and a seasonal vegetable dish. Dham is a great way to try a variety of Kinnauri dishes.

  • Thukpa: This noodle soup is a Tibetan dish that has become popular in Kinnaur. It is typically made with vegetables and meat, and can be customized to your spice preference.

  • Red Apples: Kinnaur is famous for its delicious red apples. Be sure to try some fresh apples or apple products, such as jam or juice, while you are visiting.

  • Tudkiya Bhath: A type of pulao, Tudkiya Bhath is made with rice cooked with lentils, potatoes, onions, ginger, garlic and yoghurt and spiced with bay leaf, cardamom, cinnamon, red chilli powder etc. This spicy rice dish is usually served with mash daal.
